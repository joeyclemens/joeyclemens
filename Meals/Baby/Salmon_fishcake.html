Mini Salmon & Sweet Potato Fishcakes

Soft, lightly golden patties packed with omega-3s — easy to grip, easy to gum.

Prep 10 min
Cook 20 min
Makes 12
Age 12m+

A brilliant batch-cook staple for babies and toddlers — naturally soft, no added salt, and packed with good fats. Make a big batch, freeze, and pull out as needed.

Ingredients

Makes 12 fishcakes
  • 250 gsweet potato, peeled and cubed
  • 200 gtinned salmon in water, drained — no added salt
  • 3 tbspplain flour, for dusting
  • 1large egg, beaten
  • 1 tbspfresh parsley or chives, finely chopped
  • 1 tbspolive oil, for frying

At a Glance

✓ No added salt ✓ 12 months+ ✓ Finger food Freezer-friendly Omega-3 rich Batch cook
⚠ Salt & bones — important Always use no-added-salt tinned salmon, and check carefully for any small bones before mixing. Add no extra salt — babies under 2 need very little sodium.

Serve with

Soft avocado slices, steamed broccoli florets, or strips of soft-cooked carrot all pair well and make a complete little meal.

Method

  1. 1
    Cook the sweet potato

    Boil or steam 250 g sweet potato cubes for 12–15 minutes until completely tender. Drain and mash until smooth. Leave to cool slightly.

    ⏱ 12–15 minutes
  2. 2
    Mix the fishcakes

    Flake the tinned salmon into the mash, checking carefully for any bones. Add the chopped herbs and mix well. The mixture should be firm enough to shape — if too soft, add a little extra flour.

  3. 3
    Shape and coat

    Divide into 12 small patties, roughly 4 cm wide — a good size for little hands. Dip each one in beaten egg, then dust lightly with flour.

  4. 4
    Fry until golden

    Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat. Cook the fishcakes for 3–4 minutes each side until lightly golden and heated through. Cool completely before serving.

    ⏱ 3–4 minutes per side

Notes & Variations

Freezing: Freeze after frying in a single layer, then transfer to a bag. Reheat from frozen in the oven at 180°C for 10 minutes until piping hot throughout — cool before serving.

Veg boost: Add a small handful of finely grated courgette or carrot to the mix — squeeze out excess moisture first.

For older children and adults: A squeeze of lemon juice and a pinch of black pepper lifts the flavour nicely. Keep seasoning separate so the baby batch stays salt-free.
